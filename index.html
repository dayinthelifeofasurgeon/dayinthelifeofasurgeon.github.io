Do you want to be a Surgeon?

Have you got what it takes?

Being a surgeon is stressful, fast-paced and tiring. Quick decisions are vital and you need to cope with severe pressure. You need to be calm, decisive and skilful and stay at the forefront of cutting-edge medical technology.

A typical day...

You head to work at your local NHS hospital, and today you're working the 6pm evening shift.

You start the day by examining a cancer patient who has recently had life-saving surgery. She is recovering very well, and you are happy to inform her she'll be discharged from hospital in the next few days.

Moments later, you are called to perform an emergency operation on a car-accident victim. Surgery involves many hours of complicated repairs to internal tissues and the patient looks set to make a good recovery.

Almost every day is fast-paced, stressful and tiring, but nothing is more rewarding than saving lives.
